## What Is UX/UI Design?

Let's clarify the terms that many confuse:

### UX Design (User Experience)

UX design focuses on **how a product works**—the overall experience a user has when interacting with your website or application.

**UX includes:**
- User research and personas
- Information architecture
- User flows and journeys
- Wireframing
- Usability testing
- Interaction design

**Good UX feels:** Intuitive, effortless, logical

### UI Design (User Interface)

UI design focuses on **how a product looks**—the visual elements users interact with.

**UI includes:**
- Visual design and aesthetics
- Color palettes and typography
- Icons and imagery
- Buttons and interactive elements
- Responsive layouts
- Animation and micro-interactions

**Good UI feels:** Beautiful, polished, professional

### The Codebrand Approach

1. **Research first** - Understand users before designing
2. **Function drives form** - UX decisions inform UI choices

## UX/UI Design Services Explained

### User Research & Discovery

**What it is:** Understanding your users through interviews, surveys, analytics, and competitive analysis.

**Why it matters:** Design without research is guessing. Research reveals what users actually need, not what you assume they need.

**Codebrand approach:**
- Stakeholder interviews
- User interviews and surveys
- Analytics deep-dive
- Competitive landscape analysis
- User persona development

### Information Architecture

**What it is:** Organizing content and functionality in a logical, intuitive structure.

**Why it matters:** Users should find what they need without thinking. Poor IA causes frustration and abandonment.

**Codebrand approach:**
- Sitemap creation
- Content hierarchy
- Navigation design
- User flow mapping

### Wireframing & Prototyping

**What it is:** Low-fidelity sketches and interactive prototypes to test concepts before visual design.

**Why it matters:** Catch problems early when changes are cheap. Validate ideas with real users.

**Codebrand approach:**
- Low-fidelity wireframes
- Interactive Figma prototypes
- User testing with prototypes
- Iteration based on feedback

### Visual Design & UI

**What it is:** The visual layer—colors, typography, imagery, components.

**Why it matters:** Visual design creates emotional connection and trust. It's your brand's digital expression.

**Codebrand approach:**
- Brand-aligned aesthetics
- Modern design trends (tastefully applied)
- Accessibility-first color choices
- Typography that enhances readability
- Micro-interactions that delight

### Design Systems

**What it is:** A comprehensive system of reusable components, patterns, and guidelines.

**Why it matters:** Consistency, efficiency, and scalability. Your product stays cohesive as it grows.

**Codebrand approach:**
- Component libraries in Figma
- Documented patterns
- Style guides
- Developer-ready specifications

### Usability Testing

**What it is:** Testing designs with real users to identify friction and opportunities.

**Why it matters:** Users interact differently than designers expect. Testing reveals reality.

**Codebrand approach:**
- Task-based testing
- Think-aloud protocols
- Session recordings analysis
- Actionable recommendations
