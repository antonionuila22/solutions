<h2 id="types-of-partners">Types of Web Development Partners: Pros and Cons</h2>

Before diving deeper, let's clarify your options and when each makes sense.

### Freelancers

**Best for:** Simple projects under $5,000, tight budgets, flexibility needs

**Pros:**
- Lower hourly rates ($25-100/hr)
- Direct communication
- Flexible scheduling

**Cons:**
- Single point of failure (what if they get sick?)
- Limited skill range
- No backup or support structure
- Quality highly variable

**Verdict:** Fine for small updates, risky for serious business projects.

### Large US Agencies (100+ employees)

**Best for:** Enterprise companies with $100,000+ budgets, complex integrations

**Pros:**
- Full-service capabilities
- Established processes
- Brand recognition

**Cons:**
- Extremely expensive ($150-300/hr)
- Your project may get junior developers
- Bureaucratic, slow processes
- You're a small fish in a big pond

**Verdict:** Overkill for most businesses. You pay for overhead, not results.

### Offshore Development (India, Philippines, Eastern Europe)

**Best for:** Highly technical projects with clear specs, very limited budgets

**Pros:**
- Lowest costs ($15-40/hr)
- Large talent pools

**Cons:**
- 10-12 hour timezone difference
- Communication challenges
- Quality control issues
- Cultural/business practice differences

**Verdict:** Can work for specific technical tasks, but risky for full projects.

### Nearshore Development (Latin America) ✓ RECOMMENDED

**Best for:** US businesses wanting quality at reasonable prices with easy collaboration

**Pros:**
- Competitive rates ($40-80/hr)
- Same or similar timezone (CST/EST)
- Cultural alignment with US business
- High English proficiency
- Growing tech ecosystems
- Easy travel for in-person meetings

**Cons:**
- Smaller talent pool than offshore
- Some agencies still developing processes

**Verdict:** The sweet spot for most US businesses. **This is where Codebrand operates.**

9. **No discovery phase** — Building without understanding = failure
